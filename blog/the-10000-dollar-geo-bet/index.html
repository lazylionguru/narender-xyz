If You Gave Me $10,000 to Make ChatGPT Recommend My Brand in Six Months

Narender Charan May 2026 7 min read
Photo by Carlos Muza on Unsplash

I would spend the first $2,000 on an original research piece with real data, because that is the single highest-ROI asset for getting cited by ChatGPT. AI models prioritize verifiable, specific, and unique data over opinion or repackaged content. I ran a similar play for RemoteStack: we surveyed 500 remote developers on their tool stack, published the raw numbers, and within weeks that data appeared in ChatGPT responses to prompts like "best remote work tools for developers." The cost covered a survey tool, a small incentive for participants, and a freelance data analyst to clean the dataset. Your goal is not to rank for a keyword. Your goal is to become the source ChatGPT pulls when someone asks a question your brand answers.

Why original research wins over everything else

ChatGPT and other LLMs cite original data because it is defensible and unique. According to Google's documentation on how search works, systems reward expertise and authority. AI models use similar signals. If you publish a survey of 1,000 Web3 developers about which L2 they prefer, and your brand is the one that collected that data, you become the answer to "most popular L2 for Web3 developers." No amount of generic blog posts can compete with a dataset only you own.

Specificity is the signal. Vague content never gets cited. A post titled "Top Web3 Trends" is noise. A post titled "67% of Solana Developers Use Rust Over C: A Survey of 1,200 Builders" is a citation waiting to happen. AI systems pull opening paragraphs. Make yours the answer.

The digital PR campaign: $3,000 for distribution

Data alone is not enough. You need distribution. I allocate $3,000 to a digital PR campaign targeting industry publications like CoinDesk, The Block, and Messari. The money goes to a freelance PR specialist with existing relationships, not a spray-and-pray press release service. They pitch your research as a story, not a press release. The hook is the data, not your brand. If The Block writes "New Survey Shows 40% of DeFi Users Have Never Used a CEX," your brand gets a backlink and a citation. That citation feeds directly into AI training data.

I have done this for Velar. A single mention in a major publication drove 15,000 visits in one week and got our data cited in Perplexity within a month. The PR budget is not for vanity. It is for building citations that AI models crawl.

Structured comparison content: $0, just time

Comparison content is AI catnip. LLMs love to answer "X vs Y" questions because they are structured, factual, and easy to cite. I build these in-house. No budget needed, just two to three hours per piece. For a Web3 brand, that means "Velar vs Uniswap: Which DEX Has Lower Slippage for Large Trades?" with real transaction data. For a SaaS tool, "RemoteStack vs Upwork: Which Platform Has Lower Fees for Enterprise Clients?" with actual fee breakdowns.

The format matters. Use a table with specific rows: fees, payout speed, support quality, user ratings. AI models extract these tables for answers. I have seen comparison posts from a zero-DA domain get cited by ChatGPT for "best freelance platforms for developers" because the table was the most complete public comparison available.

Directory and review platform presence: $500

This is the cheapest signal you can buy. $500 goes to listing your brand on every relevant directory, review platform, and comparison site in your niche. For Web3: DeFi Llama, DappRadar, CoinGecko, CryptoCompare. For SaaS: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Product Hunt. Each listing is an entity signal. AI models scrape these platforms for credibility signals. A G2 review with 20 real reviews and a 4.5 rating is worth more than a dozen blog posts.

I spend $0 on fake reviews. I spend $500 on a virtual assistant to complete 50 listings with accurate NAP (name, address, phone), consistent descriptions, and category selections. Consistency across platforms builds entity authority. According to Moz's research on entity-based SEO, consistent citations across the web directly improve trust signals.

Reddit community presence: $0, time only

Reddit is credibility, not a backlink channel. I spend zero dollars and two hours per week on Reddit. I do not post links. I answer questions in r/CryptoCurrency, r/DeFi, r/Web3, and relevant subreddits with specific, data-backed answers. When someone asks "Which DEX has the lowest fees for ETH swaps?" I answer with real numbers and name my brand only if it is the best answer. Reddit comments get indexed by AI training crawlers. A well-sourced Reddit comment can become a cited source in a ChatGPT answer.

The key is being helpful without being promotional. If your brand is the right answer, the community will upvote it. That upvote is a trust signal AI models respect. I grew Velar's Reddit presence from zero to consistent mentions in top comments over four months with zero budget.

Entity building across platforms: $0, time

Entity before content. Be a verifiable, consistent entity across the web. I spend time ensuring my brand has the same name, logo, description, and URL on every platform: Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, Medium, YouTube, Discord, Telegram. AI models build knowledge graph entries from these signals. If your brand appears as "Velar" on Twitter, "Velar Protocol" on LinkedIn, and "Velar DEX" on GitHub, you confuse the entity. Pick one name and use it everywhere.

I also claim my brand's Wikipedia page if eligible. A Wikipedia citation is the strongest entity signal. If not eligible, I contribute to existing Wikipedia pages in my niche and cite my own brand's data as a source. Wikipedia editors will remove self-promotion, but they keep verifiable data.

Technical GEO foundations: $0, time

Technical SEO is the foundation. GEO is the building on top. I spend time on three things: fast page load speed, proper schema markup (Organization, Product, FAQ, HowTo), and clean URL structure. According to Ahrefs' guide to schema markup, structured data helps search engines and AI models understand your content. I also add a /geo/ sitemap that lists pages optimized for AI citation: original research, comparison tables, and data-heavy posts.

No budget needed. A free Google Search Console account and a few hours of configuration are enough. Technical GEO is not optional. If your site loads in 6 seconds, ChatGPT will not wait.

The one guest post: $2,000 on a high-authority domain

With the remaining $2,000, I buy one guest post on a domain with DR 70+ and real traffic. Not a link farm. A single post on a site like CoinDesk, TechCrunch, or Forbes (if you can get an editor to accept it). The post must be a data-driven, opinionated piece that naturally mentions your brand and links to your original research. One high-authority backlink from a site that AI models trust is worth more than 50 low-quality links.

I negotiate for a permanent dofollow link with contextual anchor text. I also ask for a mention in the author bio. That bio becomes another entity signal. I have done this for RemoteStack: one guest post on a DR 75 site drove 2,000 referral visits and got our brand cited in ChatGPT answers within two weeks.

Backlinks are grunt work, not budget work. But one high-authority link is worth the budget if it comes with a data citation.

The final allocation: $10,000 total

  • Original research piece with real data: $2,000
  • Digital PR campaign targeting industry publications: $3,000
  • Structured comparison content built in-house: $0
  • Directory and review platform presence: $500
  • Reddit community presence: $0
  • Entity building across platforms: $0
  • Technical GEO foundations: $0
  • One strong guest post on high-authority domain: $2,000
  • Buffer for unexpected costs or tools: $2,500

The buffer covers tools like Ahrefs for research, a survey platform upgrade, or an extra PR retainer if the first pitch fails. Every dollar is spent on making your brand the answer, not just visible. Stop thinking in keywords. Start thinking in prompts. Reverse-engineer the AI answer. Imagine what a buyer asks ChatGPT, then be that answer.
