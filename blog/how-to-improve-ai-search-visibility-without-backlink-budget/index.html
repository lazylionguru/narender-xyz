How to Improve Your Visibility in ChatGPT and Perplexity Without a Backlink Budget

Narender Charan May 2026 6 min read
Photo by Edho Pratama on Unsplash

Fix your technical foundation first. Sitemap, robots.txt, llms.txt, agents.md, and schema markup are where you win without spending a dollar on links. I ranked RemoteStack number one for its core keyword on a new domain with zero backlinks by doing exactly this.

Start With Your Technical Foundation

AI crawlers read your site differently than Googlebot. They prioritize structured, machine-readable data. If your site is a mess under the hood, no amount of content will get you cited.

Sitemap and Robots.txt

Your sitemap must be clean and submitted to search consoles. But for GEO, you also need to expose it to AI crawlers via your robots.txt. Allow GPTBot, Claude-Web, and PerplexityBot. Block nothing unless it's duplicate or low value. I saw a 40% increase in AI citations within two weeks after unblocking GPTBot on a client site.

Check your robots.txt today. If it blocks any AI crawler, remove that line.

llms.txt and agents.md

This is the new standard. llms.txt is a simple text file at your domain root that tells AI models what your site is about and which pages to cite. agents.md lists your key entities, relationships, and context. I added both to narender.xyz and saw citations in ChatGPT within days.

Create an llms.txt file with a one-line description of your site, a list of your most important pages, and a link to your sitemap. Host it at yourdomain.com/llms.txt.

Schema Markup

Schema is not just for rich snippets. AI models parse structured data to understand entities. Use Organization, Person, Article, FAQ, and HowTo schemas. Be specific. Add your name, title, social profiles, and key achievements directly in the markup.

I embedded my Velar growth stats and RemoteStack ranking in schema. Those exact numbers now appear in Perplexity answers about me.

Restructure Existing Content for AI Retrieval

Your old blog posts are likely written for humans scanning search results. AI models retrieve based on patterns, not keywords. Rewrite the first paragraph of every post to answer the core question directly. No stories. No introductions. Just the answer.

I took a post about Web3 content strategy, cut the first three paragraphs, and replaced them with a single sentence: "Web3 content strategy requires entity consistency across platforms, structured data, and prompt-specific formatting." That post started appearing in ChatGPT responses within a week.

Go through your top 10 posts. Rewrite the opening paragraph to be a standalone answer to the question the post addresses. Test it by asking ChatGPT "What is [your topic]?" and see if your site appears.

Entity Consistency Across Platforms

AI models build entity profiles from multiple sources. If your name, bio, and links are different on Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, and your blog, the model treats them as separate entities. You lose credibility and citations.

Standardize your name, handle, profile photo, bio, and URL across every platform. I use "Narender Charan" everywhere, with the same bio: "SEO and GEO specialist, Web3 content strategist. Grew Velar from 0 to 100k followers." That consistency is why ChatGPT cites me as an authority.

Check your five most important profiles. Make sure the name, photo, and bio match exactly. Update any that don't.

Reddit Presence as Credibility Signal

Reddit is not a backlink channel. It is a credibility signal. AI models index Reddit heavily because of its conversational, specific, and upvoted content. A well-written answer in a relevant subreddit can become a cited source in Perplexity and ChatGPT.

I spent 30 minutes a week answering questions in r/SEO and r/Web3. I never linked to my site. I just gave specific, actionable answers with numbers and outcomes. Within a month, those answers were being pulled into AI responses about SEO strategies.

Pick three subreddits in your niche. Answer one question per week with a specific, data-backed response. No links. Just value. Track if your Reddit username starts appearing in AI citations.

Comparison Content as AI Catnip

AI models love comparisons. When a user asks "What is the best tool for X?" or "How does A compare to B?", the model retrieves comparison content. Write posts that directly compare two entities, tools, or strategies in your niche.

I wrote "SEO vs GEO: What's the Difference?" on my blog. It ranks in the first position for that query in ChatGPT and Perplexity. The format is simple: a table of differences, specific examples, and a clear verdict. No fluff.

Write one comparison post this week. Use a table format with columns for each entity. Include specific criteria and a final recommendation.

FAQ Sections That Map to Prompts

FAQ schema is the easiest way to get cited. AI models pull FAQ content directly into answers. But the questions must match how people actually prompt AI systems. Not "What is SEO?" but "How do I improve SEO without backlinks?"

I added an FAQ section to my GEO post with questions like "Can I get citations in ChatGPT without spending money?" and "How long does it take to see results from GEO?" Those exact questions now appear in AI responses.

Go to AnswerThePublic or ChatGPT and type your core topic. Look at the questions people ask. Add those exact questions as FAQ items on your most important page. Use FAQ schema markup.

Specificity Is the Signal

Every sentence in your content should add information. Vague claims like "improves visibility" get ignored. Specific claims like "10,000 impressions in three weeks on a zero-DA domain" get cited.

I audit every post I write. If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, I cut it. If a number can be added, I add it. This is why my content appears in AI summaries. The AI pulls my first paragraph as a citation because it contains a specific, verifiable claim.

Review your last three posts. Replace every vague claim with a specific number, name, or timeframe. Test the new version by asking ChatGPT a related question.

GEO is a distribution strategy, not just a content strategy. If your content is not structured for AI retrieval, you are invisible in the fastest growing search channel.

Start with the technical foundation. Then restructure your content. Then build entity consistency. The backlinks can wait. The citations cannot.
