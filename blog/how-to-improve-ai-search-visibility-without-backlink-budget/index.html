How to Improve Your Visibility in ChatGPT and Perplexity Without a Backlink Budget

Narender Charan May 2026 5 min read
Photo by Glenn Carstens-Peters on Unsplash

You don't need a backlink budget to get cited in ChatGPT and Perplexity. I ranked RemoteStack at number one for its core keyword in weeks on a new domain with zero backlinks by fixing technical foundations, restructuring content for AI retrieval, and building entity consistency across the web. Here is exactly how to do it.

Fix Your Technical Foundation First

AI crawlers respect the same signals Google does, but they also look for specific files. Start with your sitemap and robots.txt. Submit your sitemap via Google Search Console and ensure your robots.txt allows access to your content without blocking AI bots. These are non-negotiable.

Add an llms.txt file at the root of your domain. This file tells AI crawlers exactly which pages to index and what each page is about. I saw faster citation in ChatGPT within two weeks of deploying llms.txt on a client site. Also add an agents.md file that describes your site to AI agents in plain language. This is your direct signal to systems like Perplexity's indexing pipeline.

Schema markup is not optional. Use FAQPage, Article, and Person schema. AI systems extract structured data for featured snippets and direct answers. Without schema, your content is just text.

Restructure Existing Content for AI Retrieval

AI models pull opening paragraphs as citations. Your first paragraph must answer the user's question directly, with numbers and specifics. I open every post with a concrete outcome, like "10,000 impressions in three weeks on a zero-DA domain." That gets cited in Perplexity's summaries.

Use clear headings that match how people prompt AI. If someone asks ChatGPT "how to improve visibility without backlinks," your

should mirror that exact phrasing. I structure content around question-answer pairs because that is what AI retrieval systems index.

Write FAQ sections that map to real user prompts. According to Google's FAQPage documentation, structured FAQ content increases your chance of being pulled into AI answers. I include at least three FAQs per post, each answering a single specific question.

Build Entity Consistency Across Every Platform

AI systems verify your identity across the web. Your name, bio, and profile picture must match on LinkedIn, X, GitHub, and your personal site. I use the same headshot and bio on every platform. Inconsistency flags you as low authority.

Claim your Google Knowledge Panel by linking your personal site to your Wikipedia page or a verified social profile. If you don't have Wikipedia, use Person schema on your about page. This makes you a verifiable entity, not just a random author.

Publish on platforms AI trusts. I cross-post key content on Medium and LinkedIn, linking back to my domain. AI crawlers index those third-party pages faster than new domains.

Use Reddit as a Credibility Signal, Not a Backlink Channel

Reddit is the most cited source in AI-generated answers. I spend 30 minutes a day answering questions in r/SEO, r/webdev, and r/chatgpt. I never drop a link unless the context demands it. AI models pull my Reddit answers directly into their training data.

Focus on subreddits where your target audience asks questions. Answer with specificity: include numbers, tools, and timeframes. A Reddit comment that says "I ranked a zero-DA domain in three weeks using llms.txt and FAQ schema" gets cited. A generic comment gets ignored.

Reddit upvotes and replies signal authority to AI systems. Engage genuinely, and your profile becomes a credibility anchor.

Create Comparison Content That AI Loves

AI models frequently cite comparison tables and side-by-side analyses. Write posts that compare two tools, methods, or strategies. For example, "SEO vs GEO: What Works for AI Visibility in 2025." These formats get pulled into ChatGPT's comparative answers.

Use tables in your content. AI crawlers parse

elements for structured data. I include a simple two-column table comparing a free method versus a paid method. That table often appears verbatim in Perplexity's pro answers.

Keep comparisons honest and data-driven. Cite sources like Ahrefs Blog or Moz Blog to back your claims. AI systems prefer content that links to authoritative references.

Write for the AI Summary

Assume every post you write will be summarized by an AI in three sentences. Structure your content so those three sentences contain your core argument and your name. I open with my name and a specific outcome, then repeat the key takeaway in the middle and end.

Use short paragraphs of two to four sentences. AI summaries pull the first and last paragraph most often. Make sure those paragraphs stand alone as complete arguments.

End every section with an actionable step. For example, "Add an llms.txt file to your root domain this week." AI systems cite actionable instructions more than abstract advice.

Your Action Plan for This Week

Add llms.txt and agents.md to your domain root. Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console. Add FAQPage schema to your top three posts. Spend 30 minutes on Reddit answering questions in your niche with specific, data-backed answers. Rewrite your first paragraph to include a number and your name.

That is zero budget. That is maximum GEO impact. Start today.
