10 Questions to Ask Before Hiring a GEO Specialist

Narender Charan May 2026 6 min read
Photo by Igor Omilaev on Unsplash

Hire someone who can prove they have done this before, not someone who just read about it. I have ranked a zero-backlink domain to number one for its core keyword, grown a brand X account to 100k followers, and earned organic citations in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. These ten questions will separate the practitioners from the theorists.

1. What brands are winning in AI search right now, and why?

Strong answer: The candidate names three specific brands, explains each win in terms of entity authority, structured data, and content depth. For example, Zapier wins because its documentation is the canonical answer for automation workflows across every major LLM. They cite concrete examples like "Zapier appears in 80% of GPT responses for workflow automation queries."

Weak answer: Generic names like "Nike" or "Amazon" with no explanation. No specific GPT citation examples. Avoid anyone who cannot name a brand outside their own portfolio.

2. If you had zero backlink budget, how would you improve visibility in ChatGPT and Perplexity?

Strong answer: They describe a three-pronged approach: create comparison and listicle content that directly answers prompt-style questions, publish on Reddit and Quora to build entity signals, and optimize existing pages for featured snippets. I did exactly this with RemoteStack, ranking it number one with zero backlinks by targeting prompt-like queries.

Weak answer: "I would focus on social media" or "I would write more blog posts." No mention of prompt engineering or entity distribution. Red flag.

3. What content types are most likely to be cited by LLMs?

Strong answer: Comparison pages, step-by-step guides, listicles, and FAQ-style content. LLMs love structured data and direct answers. They cite a specific example: "A 'X vs Y' comparison page on our site was cited by Perplexity for 12 different queries."

Weak answer: "All content can be cited" or vague statements about quality. No mention of entity-specific content like glossaries or tool comparisons.

4. How do you measure GEO success when AI referral traffic is hard to track?

Strong answer: They track brand mention volume in LLM outputs using tools like Brand24 or manual spot checks, monitor direct traffic spikes to high-authority pages, and use custom UTM parameters on comparison content. They set a baseline and measure growth in citation frequency monthly.

Weak answer: "We can't really measure it" or "Just look at organic traffic." If they cannot define a proxy metric, they have never done this work.

5. What does your first 90-day GEO roadmap look like?

Strong answer: Month one: audit existing content for entity gaps and prompt alignment. Month two: publish 10 comparison and listicle pieces targeting high-volume AI prompts. Month three: distribute on Reddit, Quora, and industry forums to build entity signals. They give specific weekly deliverables, not vague phases.

Weak answer: "I will analyze and optimize." No timeline, no deliverables, no mention of distribution. Run.

6. What role do entities play in modern SEO and AI search?

Strong answer: They explain that entities are the foundation: a consistent name, logo, description, and social profiles across the web make you citable. They mention Schema.org markup, Wikipedia presence, and Crunchbase as signals. I used entity consistency to get RemoteStack cited by Gemini within weeks.

Weak answer: "Entities are keywords" or no understanding of structured data. This is a hard requirement.

7. What on our site would you remove, merge, rewrite, or expand first?

Strong answer: They identify specific pages by URL, explain why they are weak (thin content, outdated stats, no prompt value), and propose a concrete action: "Merge your three tool comparison posts into one canonical page, expand the FAQ section, and delete the 2019 case study with no traffic."

Weak answer: "I need to see the site first." A good candidate will have already reviewed your site before the interview and come with a list.

8. How do you make our brand the source AI systems quote instead of competitors?

Strong answer: They focus on becoming the most cited source for a specific entity cluster. For example, if you are a CRM tool, they would target every prompt about "best CRM for small business" with comparison content, Reddit mentions, and Schema markup. They cite a competitor they displaced and how.

Weak answer: "We will write better content." No mention of distribution, entity building, or competitive displacement.

9. Which third-party sites would you want us mentioned on in the next six months?

Strong answer: They name specific sites like G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, relevant subreddits, and industry-specific directories. They explain why each matters for entity signals and LLM citation likelihood. "I would target a Product Hunt launch and a G2 review campaign because both sites are frequently scraped by LLMs."

Weak answer: "Any high-DR site" or no specific names. They need to understand that Reddit is credibility, not a backlink channel.

10. If you were launching our company from scratch today knowing AI search exists, what would you do differently?

Strong answer: They would build a content strategy around prompt-based queries from day one, prioritize comparison and FAQ content over traditional blog posts, and invest in entity distribution (Reddit, Quora, directories) before backlinks. They would also structure the site with Schema markup and a clear entity hierarchy.

Weak answer: "I would do the same thing but with AI." No strategic shift, no understanding that GEO requires a different content philosophy.

The best GEO specialists think like the AI they are trying to influence. They reverse-engineer the prompt, build the entity, and distribute the signal. Everything else is noise.

Bonus question: If given $10,000 with one KPI — maximize the probability ChatGPT recommends our brand in six months — how would you spend it?

Strong answer: $4,000 on creating 10 high-quality comparison and listicle pages targeting the top 20 AI prompts in your niche. $3,000 on Reddit and Quora distribution (ads and manual posting). $2,000 on Schema markup and entity cleanup (Crunchbase, Wikipedia, G2). $1,000 on monitoring tools to track citation growth. They allocate every dollar to direct citation drivers, not vanity metrics.

Weak answer: "I would hire a PR agency" or "I would buy backlinks." No understanding that GEO rewards specificity and distribution, not authority alone.
