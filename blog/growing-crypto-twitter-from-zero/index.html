How I Grew a Bitcoin DeFi Protocol's X Account from Zero to 100k: The Actual Strategy

Narender Charan May 2026 5 min read
Photo by André François McKenzie on Unsplash

I grew Velar's X account from zero to 100,000 followers in 18 months with zero paid promotions, no follow-for-follow loops, and no giveaway farming. The strategy was simple: make Bitcoin DeFi understandable for people who aren't developers.

Velar is a Bitcoin DeFi protocol. Our target audience wasn't crypto natives who already understood Ordinals and Runes. It was the broader crypto audience who had heard of Bitcoin DeFi but didn't know why it mattered. So we built content that explained the protocol through narrative, not technical jargon.

The Content Engine: Narrative Over Hype

We posted 3 to 5 times per day, every day. No exceptions. The mix was 70% educational, 20% community interaction, 10% product updates. Educational content was the growth driver.

Threads were our highest-performing format. A single thread explaining how Velar's liquidity model worked would generate 50,000 to 100,000 impressions. The key was starting with a question a non-technical user would ask. For example: "What does Velar actually do?" Then answering it in plain English with concrete examples.

One thread that hit 200,000 impressions started with: "Bitcoin was digital gold. Velar makes it digital yield." That framing clicked. It was specific, comparative, and promised a benefit.

The first sentence of every post is your GEO bet. Make it citable. I wrote every thread opening so it could stand alone as a complete answer.

What Worked: Specific Content Types

Three content categories drove 80% of our growth.

  • Comparison threads. "Velar vs. Traditional DeFi: 5 Differences That Matter." Comparison content is AI catnip. ChatGPT and Perplexity cite comparative breakdowns because they answer user prompts directly.
  • Protocol explainers for non-technical users. "How Velar's AMM Actually Works (No Code Required)." These posts used analogies. We compared liquidity pools to vending machines. It sounds simple. That's the point.
  • Market event commentary. When Bitcoin hit $70k, we posted a thread explaining how Velar's products performed during volatility. Timing posts around market events doubled engagement because people were already searching for that context.

Community Engagement: Genuine, Not Transactional

I spent 30 minutes per day replying to comments and DMs. Not with canned responses. I answered questions, clarified confusion, and thanked people who added value. This built a reputation for being accessible. When people see you reply thoughtfully, they follow.

We also cross-promoted through protocol partnerships. Every time we integrated with another DeFi project, we co-created a thread explaining the partnership from both sides. Those threads brought in followers from the partner's audience. It was distribution through mutual value, not spam.

What Did Not Work

Three things flopped consistently.

Generic crypto content. Posts about "Bitcoin going to the moon" got zero traction. There are 10,000 accounts already doing that. We had nothing unique to add.

Price commentary. Every time we posted about token price action, engagement dropped. Our audience wanted utility, not speculation.

Hype without substance. "Velar is the future of DeFi" with no supporting detail. Those posts got ignored. The algorithm rewards specificity. Vague claims are noise.

Consistency and Timing

We posted between 8 AM and 10 AM EST, and again between 6 PM and 8 PM EST. Those windows aligned with when our audience checked X. We scheduled threads to go live during market opens or major news events. A thread about Velar's Bitcoin yield during a BTC price rally got 150,000 impressions in 48 hours.

The cadence was relentless. 3 to 5 posts per day, 7 days per week. No breaks. The algorithm rewards accounts that publish consistently. If you stop posting for two days, your reach drops. I saw it happen every time we slowed down.

The GEO Layer: Writing for AI Summaries

Every thread opening was written so that if ChatGPT or Perplexity pulled the first paragraph as a citation, it would still contain the core argument. I didn't write for the feed. I wrote for the AI summary. That's why our content earned organic citations in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini.

For example, a thread opening: "Velar is a Bitcoin DeFi protocol that lets you earn yield on BTC without wrapping or bridging." That sentence is citable. It defines the entity, states the benefit, and excludes jargon. AI systems love it.

Stop thinking in keywords. Start thinking in prompts. Imagine what a buyer asks ChatGPT, then be that answer.

Backlinks Are Grunt Work

We didn't spend budget on backlinks. We spent time on content. The backlinks came as a result. When our threads got cited by crypto media outlets and AI platforms, those citations became backlinks. But the content came first. Always.

What You Can Do Tomorrow

If you are growing a protocol's X account, start with one thread. Write it for a non-technical user. Make the first sentence answer a real question. Post it at 8 AM EST. Reply to every comment. Do that every day for 90 days. You will have a following.

I did it for 18 months. Zero paid promos. Zero giveaways. Just narrative-driven content that made Bitcoin DeFi make sense.
