How I Grew a Bitcoin DeFi Protocol's X Account from Zero to 100k: The Actual Strategy

Narender Charan May 2026 6 min read
Photo by Ben Sweet on Unsplash

I grew Velar's X account from zero to 100,000 followers in 18 months without spending a dollar on ads, giveaways, or follow-for-follow schemes. The playbook was simple: treat every post as an answer to a question a Bitcoin DeFi user would ask ChatGPT, then make that answer specific enough to get cited.

The Problem with Most Crypto X Strategies

Most crypto projects post price commentary, hype announcements, and retweet partners. That content gets zero engagement because it adds nothing to the reader's understanding. I tested this myself. For the first three months at Velar, I tried generic alpha posts and market updates. Impressions hovered around 200 per post. No follows, no replies, no DMs from potential users.

The audience doesn't care what your token price is doing. They care about how your protocol works and why it matters.

Narrative Driven Content Explaining the Protocol to Non Technical Users

Velar is a Bitcoin DeFi protocol. That means it lets people use Bitcoin for lending, borrowing, and trading without wrapping it or trusting a custodian. The technical reality is complex. The narrative does not have to be.

I wrote every post as if explaining the protocol to a friend who owns Bitcoin but has never used DeFi. No jargon without explanation. No acronyms without spelling them out. Every thread started with a simple question: "What happens when you deposit BTC into Velar?" or "Why would you borrow against Bitcoin instead of selling it?"

One thread explaining how smart contracts enable trustless Bitcoin lending got 12,000 impressions and 400 new followers in 48 hours. It was a 12 post thread with a diagram in post three and a worked example in post six. No hype. Just a clear answer to a real question.

Consistent Cadence: 3 to 5 Posts Per Day

I posted three to five times per day, seven days a week. No breaks. No scheduling gaps. The algorithm rewards consistency, and the audience expects it. I used a content calendar with four buckets: educational threads, protocol updates, community highlights, and market context posts.

Educational threads got the most saves and shares. Protocol updates got the most quote retweets. Community highlights built social proof. Market context posts tied Velar to broader Bitcoin narratives without being price commentary.

Thread Format for Technical Explainers

Long form threads outperformed single posts by a factor of 10x on average. A single post about Velar's liquidation mechanism would get 300 impressions. A seven post thread on the same topic would get 4,000 impressions and 50 bookmarks.

I structured every thread the same way: post one hooks with a question or counterintuitive statement, posts two through six build the explanation step by step, and post seven ends with a clear takeaway and a call to follow for more. Each post was self contained so readers could jump in anywhere.

Genuine Community Engagement

I replied to every reply in the first hour. Not automated. Not copy pasted. Real answers to real questions. If someone asked "how does Velar compare to a CEX?" I wrote a three sentence comparison right there in the reply. If someone said "this is confusing" I asked what part and explained it again.

This built a reputation. People started tagging Velar in threads about Bitcoin DeFi because they knew I would show up and answer. That organic tagging drove more impressions than any hashtag strategy ever could.

Timing Posts Around Market Events

When Bitcoin price dropped 15% in a day, I posted a thread titled "Why smart money borrows against Bitcoin during dips instead of selling." It got 8,000 impressions in three hours. When the SEC approved a spot Bitcoin ETF, I posted a thread explaining how Velar gives retail users the same yield opportunities that institutions get through ETFs. That thread got picked up by a crypto newsletter with 50,000 subscribers.

Market events create attention. Your job is to channel that attention toward your protocol's specific value proposition.

Cross Promotion Through Protocol Partnerships

I identified five complementary Bitcoin DeFi protocols and proposed co authored threads. We wrote one thread together comparing our approaches to Bitcoin yield. Both sides posted it, both sides tagged each other, and both sides gained followers from the other's audience. Each co authored thread added between 500 and 1,500 new followers.

The key is genuine complementarity. Not random collabs. Partner with protocols that serve the same user in a different way.

What Did Not Work

Generic crypto content flopped. I posted a thread about "top 5 DeFi trends in 2024" and it got 200 impressions. Zero specificity, zero connection to Velar, zero reason for anyone to care.

Price commentary also flopped. Every time I posted about Bitcoin price action without tying it back to Velar's product, engagement dropped. The audience comes for the protocol, not the price.

Hype without substance was the biggest waste of time. Announcements like "We are building something huge" or "Big news coming soon" got zero engagement and annoyed existing followers. I stopped posting teasers entirely after the second month.

Specificity Is the Signal

The single highest performing post I ever wrote was a 500 word thread answering "What happens to your Bitcoin if Velar gets hacked?" It addressed the core fear of every DeFi user with specific details about the protocol's insurance fund, audit history, and smart contract architecture. It got 18,000 impressions, 2,000 likes, and 900 bookmarks.

Vague content gets ignored. Specific content gets cited, saved, and shared. According to Moz's research on specificity in content, pages with concrete examples and specific numbers rank higher and earn more backlinks. The same principle applies on X.

Reverse Engineering the AI Answer

I started every content idea by asking myself: "If a Bitcoin user asks ChatGPT 'how do I earn yield on my Bitcoin without selling it,' what answer do I want ChatGPT to give?" Then I wrote that answer as a thread. This is Generative Engine Optimization applied to social media.

When Perplexity and ChatGPT started citing Velar's documentation in their answers to Bitcoin DeFi questions, I knew the strategy was working. The same content that grew the X account also earned organic citations in AI systems.

Stop thinking in keywords. Start thinking in prompts. If you want to be the answer, write the answer.

What You Can Do Tomorrow

Pick one question your ideal user asks Google or ChatGPT. Write a seven post thread that answers it completely. No hype. No fluff. Just the answer. Post it. Reply to every comment. Then do it again the next day. That is the entire strategy.
