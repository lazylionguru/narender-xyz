The 90-Day GEO Roadmap: What I Do in the First Three Months

Narender Charan May 2026 4 min read
Photo by Merakist on Unsplash

I spend the first 90 days on three things: technical foundations, content restructuring, and authority building. Each month has a specific focus with a clear done state. Here is exactly what that looks like.

Month 1: Technical Foundations

I start with the files that AI crawlers and search engines actually read. sitemap.xml goes first. I generate it, submit it to Google Search Console, and verify the domain. Done state: every page is indexed within 10 days.

Next is robots.txt. I block staging environments, duplicate paths, and admin panels. Then I add llms.txt and agents.md. These are not standard yet, but Perplexity and GPT crawl them. I list my core pages and a one-sentence summary per page. Done state: the files exist at /llms.txt and /agents.md and return 200.

Schema markup comes next. I apply Organization, Article, and FAQPage schemas to every relevant page. I use the JSON-LD format and test with Google's Rich Results Test. Done state: zero errors, zero warnings on the homepage and top 10 pages.

I run an entity consistency audit across Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Twitter, GitHub, and any directory the brand appears on. Name, description, logo, and URL must match exactly. I log every discrepancy in a spreadsheet and fix them one by one. Done state: 100% consistency across 10+ platforms.

Finally, canonical URLs and redirects. I check for duplicate content with Screaming Frog. Every page gets a self-referencing canonical. Done state: zero duplicate content issues flagged.

Month 2: Content Restructuring

I audit every existing page against one question: does the first paragraph answer the implied question? Google's documentation on how search works confirms that opening paragraphs carry weight. If the first paragraph is vague, I rewrite it. Done state: every page has a first paragraph that could be cited as a standalone answer.

I add FAQ sections to every page. Each FAQ uses FAQPage schema. I pull questions from Google's "People also ask" box and from Reddit threads in the niche. Done state: 5 to 10 FAQ entries per page, all schema-marked.

Then I build comparison posts. AI models cite comparison content because it directly answers "which is better" queries. I write posts like "Tool X vs Tool Y: Which One Actually Works?" with a clear winner and data to back it up. Done state: three comparison posts live, each ranking for its target query within 30 days.

I start a Reddit presence. Not link dropping. I answer questions thoroughly in relevant subreddits and include the brand name naturally in the response. Reddit posts get indexed fast and cited by AI. Done state: 10 high-quality answers with positive karma and at least one mention of the brand per week.

Month 3: Authority Building

I write guest posts for sites that already appear in AI citations. I find these by searching "site:perplexity.ai [keyword]" and noting the domains. I pitch original data or a unique take. Done state: two guest posts published on domains with existing AI citations.

I list the product on Product Hunt, G2, and Alternatives.co. These platforms get crawled by AI and show up in comparison queries. I write a detailed description and include screenshots. Done state: verified profiles on all three platforms with at least 10 reviews or upvotes.

I do digital PR outreach. I find journalists covering the niche via HARO or Qwoted and offer a quote. No pitch, just a direct answer to their query. Done state: one mention in a publication with domain authority above 60.

Finally, I publish one original research piece. I survey 100 people in the industry or pull data from a public API. Ahrefs' guide to data-driven content confirms that original data gets cited at 3x the rate of opinion pieces. Done state: the research piece earns at least two organic backlinks and one AI citation within 60 days.

The first 90 days are not about traffic. They are about becoming the entity that AI cites. Traffic follows that.

Start Month 1 today. Run the entity consistency audit first. It is the cheapest and highest-impact task on this roadmap.
